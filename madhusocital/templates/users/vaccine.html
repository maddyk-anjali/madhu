Maternal Vaccinations: Ensuring the health

Introduction

Maternal immunisation involves administering vaccines during pregnancy to protect both the mother and the infant from infectious diseases. This preventive healthcare measure is crucial for reducing morbidity and mortality associated with certain infections.

Principles of Maternal Immunisation

    The primary goal of maternal immunisation is to directly protect pregnant women and to provide passive immunity to infants through the transfer of maternal antibodies across the placenta. This transfer begins around 13 weeks of gestation and increases significantly after 28 weeks.

Recommended Vaccines During Pregnancy

Vaccine Purpose Timing
TT1 – Tetanus Toxoid 1 Provides primary protection against tetanus to both mother and baby. Early pregnancy (as soon as possible)
TT2 – Tetanus Toxoid 2 Boosts immunity and ensures better protection. At least 4 weeks after TT1 (before the third trimester)
Influenza Protects pregnant women from severe influenza disease and provides passive immunity to infants. Any stage of pregnancy.
Pertussis (Whooping Cough) Protects infants from pertussis-related morbidity and mortality. Third trimester (preferably between 20 and 32 weeks).
COVID-19 Protects pregnant women from severe COVID-19 illness. Any stage of pregnancy.
Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) Protects infants from RSV-related respiratory infections. Third trimester (28-36 weeks).

Safety of Maternal Vaccinations

    >Extensive research supports the safety of administering inactivated vaccines during pregnancy. These vaccines do not pose risks to the developing fetus and are crucial for preventing serious maternal and neonatal infections.

Vaccines Contraindicated During Pregnancy

Live attenuated vaccines are generally contraindicated during pregnancy due to potential risks to the fetus. If inadvertently administered, consult with a healthcare provider for appropriate guidance.

Consultation and Planning

Pregnant women should consult with their healthcare providers to plan appropriate vaccinations during pregnancy. This ensures optimal protection for both mother and baby.
